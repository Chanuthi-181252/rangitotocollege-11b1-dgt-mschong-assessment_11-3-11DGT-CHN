Interventions

  1. Introduced predators/competitor species - creation of offshore or onshore predator free islands which have predator proof fences and are monitored with cameras, traps, tracking cards and tunnels. (Protected Areas)
  2. Restoration of habitat - replanting the natural habitat so specialist species can feed and reproduce in their original habitat.
  3. Establishing new populations in suitable habitats by translocation.
    • New populations must be large enough population so inbreeding is minimised and founder effect is not too pronounced. Enough breeding pairs must be translocated to prevent inbreeding depression.
    • Populations must be far enough apart so diseases can not be transferred between populations. If one population creashes other popuations ensure the species is not wiped out.
    • Transfer birds between populations after they are established to maintain genetic diversity and to prevent RIMs (Reproductive Isolation Mechanisms) forming (eg. behavioural due to birdsong).
    • Different populations would be exposed to different selection pressures so the allele frequencies of the populations would differ, increasing genetic diversity.
  4. Double clutching increases the number of chicks hatched increasing the reproductive rate. Surrogate species may be used to raise these chicks. The surrogate must be a closey related species as the chicks may imprint the behaviour of the parent.
  5. Hand raising chicks in captivitiy to remove risk factors to allow them to survive until adolescence when they can be released. Adolescents are more likely to survive predation.
  6. DNA screening to identify parents with deleterious alleles - selective breeding - do not allow these birds to breed. DNA screening can also identify genetically diverser individuals to increase outbreeding.
  7. Gene Editing - using Crispr to potentially edit harmful alleles from an individual/population.
  8. Wildlife corridors that maintain gene flow between populations/prevent species crossing highways where they can be killed.
  9. Legislate against any human impacts affecting the vulnerable species (eg. bn hunting etc.)
  10. Vaccinations - vaccines can be developed to help prevent spread of pathogens etc.
